## Test Results

### Test 1: Morning Brew Logo

Question:
Create a prompt for a coffee shop brand called "Morning Brew".

Bad Bot response:
The original version produced a very detailed prompt describing textures, lighting, materials, paper fibers, and photography style. While the result was creative, the explanation was very long and included unnecessary detail.

Better Bot response:
The improved version still describes a handcrafted linocut-style coffee mug logo but focuses only on the most important textures and lighting elements.

What changed:
The response became shorter and more focused while still maintaining the handcrafted aesthetic.

Verdict:
Better

### Test 2: Coffee Sleeve Branding

Better

## What Happened

Did the fix work?

Yes. Limiting the explanation helped make the prompts clearer and easier to use while still keeping the design guidance.

What else changed?

The responses became more efficient. The bot still suggests textures and materials, but avoids overwhelming the user with unnecessary detail.

Is it still "your" bot?

Yes. The bot still behaves like a knowledgeable design partner focused on authentic, handcrafted aesthetics.

## Reflection

What made you pick THIS problem over the others?

I chose this problem because the responses sometimes became too long. Reducing unnecessary explanation makes the bot easier to use while keeping its creative guidance.

How big was the change compared to its impact?

The change was small but had a noticeable impact. A simple adjustment made the prompts clearer and more practical.

Where would your Better Bot actually be useful now?

This bot would be useful for designers, marketers, and small business owners who want to generate authentic branding prompts for logos, packaging, or flyers without the typical AI-generated look.
